Guitar Effect Pedals (stompboxes)

Before I really got interested in electronics, I mainly coded a lot. Then I started building effect pedals for my electric guitar.

My school didn't have an electronics course. I was forced to discover it on my own, which I quite happily did. Apart from going through a variety of electronic hobby kits and similar sandboxed projects, I built several guitar effect pedals.

I frequently visited the amazing Tonepad website, and finally got my act together and started building boards. To date I've built 5 pedals, none of which quite followed the schematics on Tonepad.

Combo box

My first attempt at pedal building was interesting, because it was the first time I‘d ever done something this complex, and the first time I’d ever etched a board. I used a permanent marker to draw the traces, as I didn‘t have a laser printer at hand. For etching, I hijacked my school’s chemistry lab assistant and managed to get some Ferric Chloride. The final results were sketchy, but they worked, and they did what they were designed for.

I built three boards in this manner, and put them into a custom acrylic combo box with one input and one output. The effects were

  • Orange Squeezer (Compression)
  • Purple Peaker (Hi/Lo frequency booster)
  • Blue Clipper (Distortion)

Hopefully I'll have some pictures I can put up soon…
